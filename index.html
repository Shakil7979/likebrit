About Britney Gengel

Britney Gengel's Legacy of Compassion and Hope!

Britney Gengel was a compassionate and spirited 19-year-old who set out on a journey of hope to Haiti with a dream of making a difference. Tragically, she lost her life in the devastating 2010 earthquake, but not before expressing her heartfelt desire to open an orphanage for the children she had grown to love.

Her last text message that was sent to her mom 3 hours before the earthquake, ignited a powerful mission that led to the creation of Brit's Home in Haiti. Britney's legacy lives on through the lives she continues to touch, inspiring countless others to contribute and carry forward her dream.

Our Home

Welcome to Brit’s Home

Brit’s Home, a 19,000-square-foot orphanage shaped like a "B" in honor of Britney, embodies her enduring love for Haiti’s children. Built to San Francisco seismic standards, it ensures their safety in an earthquake-prone region. Inside, a family-like environment nurtures each child with food, shelter, education, and emotional support. Inspired by Britney’s vision, our dedicated staff create a loving space where every child thrives. Education at Brit’s Academy, along with recreational activities and health care, empowers children to grow resilient and compassionate. Join us in continuing Britney’s legacy and supporting these children on their path to leadership and success.

Child Sponsorship

Does International Child Sponsorship Work?

The Compassion child sponsorship program improves education by 1.03-1.46 years, boosts school completion rates, and enhances job prospects, focusing on white-collar employment. It emphasizes self-esteem and aspirations, with follow-up studies in Bolivia, Kenya, and Indonesia confirming similar benefits for children's education and career goals. This approach highlights the importance of addressing internal constraints for economic development.

Family Focused

At Brit's Home, each room accommodates five children with one dedicated caregiver. We prioritize keeping 19 sets of siblings together and maintaining connections with their living relatives. Thirty percent of our residents are genuine orphans, while others come from challenging situations with a surviving guardian. Upon turning 18, they transition to our 'Transition' compound.

Read More

Better Education

The children at Brit's Home attend Brit's Academy, where active learning is emphasized, combining comprehension with practical application. While primarily in-person, we also offer online education. Many children excel in technology, often teaching caregivers and teachers to enhance computer skills. The local education system, with literacy rates below 50%, contrasts with our innovative approach.
